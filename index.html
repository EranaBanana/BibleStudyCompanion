Getting Started

How To Use This Journal

A guide to every section. Start here, then work through the journal at your own pace.

Quick Start
01
Enter Your Name
Type your name in the header input at the top right. Your journal greets you by name and the data is saved privately in your browser.
02
Start Your Reading Tracker
Go to Reading Tracker and click any book to open its chapter grid. Tap each chapter you have read. Progress saves automatically.
03
Log a Study Session
Choose a study method (SOAP is great for beginners), fill in the passage and notes, and save. Each method has a guide at the top explaining how it works.
04
Export Your Data
Use the Export button in the footer to download a backup file. Import it on any device to restore your journal. Do this regularly.
Study Methods Explained
✦ SOAP Method — Scripture, Observation, Application, Prayer▼
The most popular structured Bible study method. Scripture: write out the key verse(s). Observation: what does the passage say? Note facts, context, what stands out. Application: what does this mean for your life right now? Prayer: respond to God in prayer based on what you read. SOAP works for any passage and any level of experience.
✦ Verse Mapping — Deep dive into a single verse▼
Choose one verse and break it apart. Look up the original Hebrew or Greek meaning of key words. Find cross-references (other verses that use the same word or theme). Note the historical and cultural context. Rewrite the verse in your own words. Verse mapping transforms a familiar verse into fresh revelation.
✦ Topical Study — Follow a theme across the Bible▼
Choose a topic (e.g. Faith, Forgiveness, the Holy Spirit) and collect all the relevant passages across both testaments. A concordance or Bible app makes this easy. Record what each passage says about the topic and draw conclusions at the end. Great for building theological depth on specific subjects.
✦ Book Overview — Understand a whole book of the Bible▼
Read through an entire book (or use a summary) and answer key questions: Who wrote it, when, and to whom? What are the major themes? How does it fit into the larger Bible story? What is the main message? Book overviews help you see the big picture before diving into chapter-by-chapter study.
✦ Word Study — Explore the original meaning of a word▼
Pick a significant word from the text and look it up in a Hebrew or Greek lexicon (Blue Letter Bible is free online). Record the original word, its definition, how many times it appears, and other verses that use it. Understanding original language meanings often unlocks entirely new dimensions of a familiar passage.
✦ Biographical Study — Study a person in the Bible▼
Choose a biblical figure and trace their story. Who were they? What were their strengths and weaknesses? What was their relationship with God? What can you learn from their life? What promises or challenges apply to your own walk? Great examples: David, Esther, Paul, Mary Magdalene, Daniel.
✦ Devotional — A shorter, reflective approach▼
Read a short passage, sit quietly with it, and ask: What is God saying to me today through this? Write your reflection — not an analysis, but a personal, honest response. What are you feeling? What are you grateful for? What does God want you to trust him with? Devotional reading is less about study and more about encounter.
Will my data be saved if I close the browser?▼
Yes — everything saves automatically to your browser's local storage. As long as you do not clear your browser data, your journal will be exactly as you left it when you return. Use the Export button regularly to back up your data to a file you can keep safely.
Can I use this on multiple devices?▼
Yes. Export your data on one device, then open the journal on another device and use Import Data to restore it. Repeat whenever you want to sync. Your data is never sent to any server — it lives entirely on your own devices.
Is my journal private?▼
Completely. All data is stored locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to Erana Banana or any third party. Your prayers, reflections, and notes are visible only to you on your own device.
